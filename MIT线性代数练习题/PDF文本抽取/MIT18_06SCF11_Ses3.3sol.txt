Problem 27.1: (6.5 #33. Introduction to Linear Algebra: Strang) When A
and B are symmetric positive deﬁnite, AB might not even be symmetric,
but its eigenvalues are still positive. Start from ABx = λx and take dot
products with Bx. Then prove λ > 0.

Solution:


                                 ABx = λx
                           ( ABx)T Bx = (λx)T Bx
                         ( Bx)T A T Bx = λx T Bx
                         ( Bx)T A( Bx) = λ(x T Bx).

where A T = A because A is symmetric. Since A is positive deﬁnite we
know ( Bx) T A( Bx) > 0, and since B is positive deﬁnite x T Bx > 0. Hence,
λ must be positive as well.
